# 3. User Personas

Eight personas, two of which are the strategic core: **Diaspora** (the unfair-advantage market) and **Organizers** (the supply engine). Every flow should be pressure-tested against these two first.

---

## 3.1 The Event Visitor — "Erisa", 24, Tirana

Marketing junior, phone-native, plans Thursday-to-Sunday around Instagram stories and friend group chats.

- **Goals:** Know what's on *tonight/this weekend* without opening five apps; not miss things her friends go to; find something when plans fall through at 21:00.
- **Frustrations:** Event info scattered across venue Instagram accounts; posters with no date/location; finding out about things after they happen; everything in English on "local" apps.
- **Motivations:** FOMO, social currency ("I found this place"), spontaneity.
- **Ideal workflow:** Opens AlbaGo → city already remembered → "Tonight" chip → 12 real options with photos and prices → taps one → sees who else is going / how busy → saves or shares to group chat in one tap. Total: 45 seconds.
- **What she needs that doesn't exist yet:** density of real events; "friends are going" signal; price/free filter she trusts; Albanian UI.

## 3.2 The Protest Participant — "Andi", 31, Tirana (and Munich, twin persona)

Engineer, politically awake since 2025, attends when he can, shares always.

- **Goals:** Reliable when/where for protests (rumors are the norm); safety info; feel part of something between protests; show solidarity from abroad if he can't attend.
- **Frustrations:** Conflicting times in Telegram forwards; fake/moved events; not knowing turnout expectations; platforms that feel like they might hand data to the wrong people.
- **Motivations:** Civic duty, anger, hope, belonging.
- **Ideal workflow:** Push notification "New protest announced in your city" → opens page: verified time, meeting point on map, expected attendance, safety panel, countdown → downloads the poster → forwards to 3 group chats → uploads his placard photo afterwards.
- **Trust requirements (non-negotiable):** no attendance tracking of individuals, no political party affiliation, moderation transparency. He is also AlbaGo's best evangelist — the share loop lives on him.

## 3.3 The Organizer — "Klea", 29, runs a techno collective in Tirana

Books DJs, promotes on Instagram, sells door tickets in cash, burns out doing everything manually.

- **Goals:** Fill the room; know if promotion is working; look professional; stop re-typing event info into five platforms; eventually sell tickets without Eventbrite's fees.
- **Frustrations:** Instagram reach collapse; no idea how many people saw her post; no attendee list; every platform wants her to rebuild her audience from zero.
- **Motivations:** Ticket revenue, reputation, creative identity.
- **Ideal workflow:** Creates event in 3 minutes on phone → AlbaGo generates the poster + story + reel automatically → publishes to AlbaGo + gets share pack for IG/WhatsApp → watches views/saves tick up on her dashboard → sees "43 people saved this, mostly 18–24 in Tirana" → posts a reminder → after the event, her follower count on AlbaGo grew, and her *next* event notifies all of them.
- **The hook that wins her:** the follower graph she owns + analytics she's never had. The hook that keeps her: every event grows the audience for the next one.

## 3.4 The Venue — "Bunker 1944 Bar", Tirana

Owner-operated, 120-cap, hosts events weekly, marketing = one overworked person with Canva.

- **Goals:** Butts in seats on weeknights; show up when tourists search "bars in Tirana"; a page that looks better than their dead website; know which events actually drew people.
- **Frustrations:** Google Business is static; Instagram is labor; no way to say "we're busy/quiet right now"; platforms charge for basics.
- **Motivations:** Revenue per night, reputation, low effort.
- **Ideal workflow:** Claims their venue page (photo, hours, vibe tags, menu link) → every event hosted there auto-appears on their page → monthly email: "2,300 people viewed your page, 480 got directions" → upgrades to Pro for a featured slot during tourist season.
- **Note:** venues are also *supply acquisition targets* — one venue brings all its events and its event organizers.

## 3.5 The Community — "Shoqata Shqiptare München" (Albanian Association of Munich)

Volunteer-run diaspora association, 800 members on a Facebook group and a WhatsApp broadcast, organizes Flag Day, concerts by touring Albanian artists, kids' language school.

- **Goals:** Reach members reliably (Facebook shows posts to 5%); look legitimate to new arrivals; manage RSVPs for a 300-person hall; recruit volunteers; preserve community identity in the second generation.
- **Frustrations:** Every tool is generic and English/German-only; membership knowledge lives in one founder's head; young Albanians aren't on Facebook.
- **Motivations:** Mission, continuity, pride.
- **Ideal workflow:** Community page on AlbaGo (bio, city, members, upcoming events, photo wall) → posts event → all followers + all AlbaGo users in Munich notified → RSVP list with headcount → volunteer signup attached → after event, photos on the community wall.
- **Strategic weight:** This persona is the §08 thesis. Win 50 of these associations and AlbaGo owns the diaspora.

## 3.6 The Business — "Vodafone Albania marketing dept" / local gym / language school

Wants attention from a defined demographic; events are a channel, not the product.

- **Goals:** Sponsor visibility on relevant events; run branded events (product launches, races); reach diaspora consumers (remittances, telecom, travel).
- **Frustrations:** No aggregated Albanian-audience digital channel exists besides buying Instagram ads.
- **Ideal workflow:** Buys a "Featured in Tirana this month" slot or sponsors a category rail ("Sports, presented by X") with clear impression reporting.
- **CPO caution:** monetization persona — relevant from month ~12, not now. Never on civic surfaces.

## 3.7 The Tourist — "Jonas", 27, German, 5 days in the Albanian Riviera

Found Albania on TikTok, planning nothing beyond a hostel booking.

- **Goals:** "What's on tonight near me" in English/German; beach parties, live music, local culture that isn't a tourist trap; not getting scammed on prices.
- **Frustrations:** Google Maps shows restaurants, not *events*; local event info is Albanian-only Instagram; Fever/Eventbrite have zero Albania inventory.
- **Ideal workflow:** Googles "events Saranda tonight" → AlbaGo event page ranks #1 (JSON-LD already in place) → opens map view → filters "Tonight · Music" → gets directions → later installs PWA for the rest of the trip.
- **Why he matters:** near-zero competition for this SEO surface, high summer volume, and he *pays* — tourist-facing inventory is the most monetizable non-civic surface.

## 3.8 The Diaspora Member — "Arlinda", 35, nurse in London, from Vlora

Sends money home, visits twice a year, aches for home daily, WhatsApp-first.

- **Goals:** Feel connected (protests, concerts, news of home); find Albanians and Albanian events in London; plan her August trip home around events; get her kids into Albanian community life.
- **Frustrations:** Diaspora life is fragmented across dozens of Facebook groups per city; she hears about the Albanian singer's London concert *after* it sells out; supporting the movement from abroad feels passive.
- **Motivations:** Identity, belonging, guilt-relief (contributing from afar), family.
- **Ideal workflow:** AlbaGo set to "London" shows Albanian events in London *and* a "Back home" rail for Vlora → follows the London Albanian community + two artists → gets a push when either posts → in July, switches city to Vlora and builds her trip itinerary from the events list → uploads her London solidarity-protest placard to Pankartat.
- **Strategic weight:** the persona with the most money, the most homesickness, and the least competition for her attention. The dual-city view ("where I live" + "back home") is the single most diaspora-native feature AlbaGo could build — no competitor has it or would think of it.

## Persona priority (where to invest, in order)

1. **Diaspora (3.8)** — the moat market; nobody else is building for her.
2. **Organizer (3.3)** — the supply engine; without Klea there is no catalog.
3. **Community (3.5)** — the durable institution layer; converts civic energy into permanence.
4. **Protest participant (3.2)** — already served; maintain trust, add alerts.
5. **Event visitor (3.1)** — served by supply, not by more features.
6. **Venue (3.4)** — supply multiplier; concierge-onboard, don't build self-serve yet.
7. **Tourist (3.7)** — SEO + summer; cheap to serve once supply exists.
8. **Business (3.6)** — year 2. Saying no to money until the audience exists.
