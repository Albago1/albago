# 10. Business Model — monetization strategies, ranked

## Principles before pricing

1. **Civic is free forever, and loudly so.** Monetizing protest infrastructure would destroy the trust that built the platform. Publish the pledge; it's a brand asset, not a cost.
2. **Free events are free to list forever.** The catalog is the product; taxing supply before demand exists is how marketplaces die. Loss-leader policy, stated publicly (anti-Eventbrite positioning).
3. **Monetize success, not access.** Charge when AlbaGo demonstrably made someone money (tickets sold, rooms filled, customers reached) — never for the basics.
4. **Sequence:** revenue work begins ~month 10–12 (per §13), after supply and retention exist. Building billing before audience is procrastination with invoices.

## Ranked strategies

### #1 — Ticketing fees (the eventual core engine)
3–5% + payment costs, vs Eventbrite's effective ~7–10%. Stripe Connect (schema already reserves for it: `stripe_account_id`, `event_tickets`, `ticket_purchases` in Phase 7E notes). Next-day payouts, free events free, fee absorbed or passed at organizer's choice.
- **Why #1:** aligned (earn when organizers earn), scales with GMV, industry-proven, and the audience-compounding promise (§7) makes the take-rate defensible.
- **Realism:** Albania is cash-at-door culture; adoption starts with diaspora-city events (card-native markets) and high-demand shows (touring artists), then normalizes at home. Kosovo/Albania card penetration is rising fast.
- **Revenue shape at maturity (year 3):** 2,000 ticketed events/yr × 150 tickets × €12 avg × 4% ≈ €150k/yr, growing with density and price points. Not a unicorn line alone — the portfolio below stacks on top.

### #2 — Venue Pro subscriptions (the SMB SaaS layer)
€19–49/mo: claimed page with analytics (views, direction-taps), photo galleries, featured placement in venue rails, weekly program tools, wallet-pass branding.
- **Why #2:** venues have marketing budgets and a permanent need (fill weeknights); subscription revenue is predictable; the free tier (claim your page) doubles as supply acquisition.
- **Gate:** needs venue pages with real traffic first (tourist season is the proof moment).

### #3 — Promoted placement (transparent, capped)
"Promoted" labeled slots in feed/city pages, self-serve, auctioned or flat-rate. Cap at ~1 in 10 slots; never in civic surfaces, never disguised as organic.
- **Why #3:** easiest to build after analytics exist; organizers already understand boosting from Instagram; margin ~100%.
- **Guardrail:** Fever's opacity is the anti-pattern — trust is the brand; label everything.

### #4 — Organizer Pro (€15–29/mo)
Advanced analytics (demographics, benchmarks), follower blasts beyond free quota, CRM export, team seats, priority review, boosted diaspora reach (notify a second city).
- **Why #4:** the report-card email (§7) is the natural upsell surface; costs are near-zero.
- **Gate:** free tier must first prove value — Pro sells the *more* of something loved.

### #5 — Diaspora business directory (unique to AlbaGo)
Albanian-owned businesses (restaurants, services, travel agencies, remittance/telecom) pay for listings/ads reaching a targeted national community across cities. €10–50/mo per listing, sold community-by-community.
- **Why ranked here:** nobody else can offer this audience; complements community pages (§8); real willingness-to-pay (these businesses currently buy Facebook ads blind).
- **Risk:** low ARPU, sales-heavy; treat as community-strengthening revenue, not a growth engine.

### #6 — Tourism & partnerships
Riviera/city guides with tourism boards, hotel/experience affiliate revenue in trip mode, "official events partner" municipal deals, co-marketed summer campaigns (the August diaspora return is a sponsorable national event in itself).
- Seasonal, BD-heavy, but high-margin and brand-building. Starts as PR, becomes revenue.

### #7 — Community payments (dues/donations processing)
Small fee (1–2%) on association membership dues and fundraisers through community pages.
- High trust sensitivity — ship late, after communities are beloved. Potentially large in diaspora (associations move real money for halls, schools, disaster relief).

### #8 — Data & insights (aggregate only)
Quarterly "state of Albanian events/nightlife" reports free (PR); custom aggregate insights for brands, municipalities, tourism bodies, paid. Never individual data, never civic data — the pledge applies absolutely.

### #9 — White-label "for-X" platform (year 3+ option)
The diaspora-nation playbook productized for other nations' communities (per §9 Japan verdict). Licensing or hosted SaaS. Optionality, not plan.

## What is explicitly rejected

- **Paid listings / listing taxes** (kills supply).
- **Selling user data or individual targeting** (kills the civic trust that is the moat).
- **Advertising networks / programmatic display** (kills the cinematic brand; the product must never look like a portal from 2009).
- **Charging attendees for discovery** (kills the loop; consumer premium only viable at scarcity scale, years away).
- **Anything on civic surfaces.** Repeated because it will be tempting during the first revenue crunch.

## The stacked model at maturity (year 3 sketch)

| Stream | Share | Character |
|---|---|---|
| Ticketing | ~45% | GMV-scaling core |
| Venue Pro + Organizer Pro | ~30% | Predictable SaaS floor |
| Promoted placement | ~15% | High-margin variable |
| Directory + tourism + partnerships | ~10% | Community/brand revenue |

Sequencing gate for all of it: **do not start revenue work until weekly active users and supply both hold a two-quarter uptrend.** Premature monetization of a thin marketplace reads as desperation and repels the exact organizers being courted (consistent with the user's own recorded principle: features without content to back them feel like noise — pricing without traffic is the same failure, with invoices).
