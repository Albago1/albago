# 7. Organizer Ecosystem — the world's best organizer platform

## The promise that wins

Every event platform makes one of two promises. Eventbrite: *"we'll process your tickets."* Facebook: *"we'll show your event to some people, maybe."* AlbaGo's promise must be different and bigger:

> **"Every event you run makes your next one easier to fill."**

That's an *audience-compounding* promise, and it's the one thing organizers can't get anywhere: Instagram rents them reach, Eventbrite keeps the customer relationship, WhatsApp doesn't scale. AlbaGo gives organizers an owned, growing, notify-able audience — plus proof it's working.

## The organizer journey, end-state design

### 1. Arrival (minute 0–5): "publish before you decide to commit"
- FB/IG event URL or street-poster photo → AI-prefilled draft (import wedge — the single best supply-acquisition feature possible, because it makes switching cost ~zero).
- One-page Luma-style creation form (title, when, where, photo → live) for simple events; the existing 8-step wizard remains for complex/recurring ones.
- Draft preview looks *gorgeous* before signup is even required; auth gate at publish, not at start (pattern already fixed in submit-event — keep it).

### 2. First publish (day 0): the "wow" moment
- Instant share pack: poster, story, reel, QR flyer PDF, bilingual caption — zero clicks, attached to the confirmation email. (All the rendering tech exists today; it's an assembly job.)
- The event page itself is the organizer's best-looking web presence — most Tirana collectives have no website. "Your event page IS your marketing site" is a real pitch.
- Immediate follow CTA on the event page starts building their audience from visitor #1.

### 3. The week of the event: the control room
- Live dashboard: views, saves, RSVPs, share-taps, geography (Tirana vs diaspora split — a datum literally no other platform can give an Albanian artist).
- One announcement blast to followers + RSVPs (rate-limited, to keep the channel sacred).
- Reminder automation: platform sends 24h/2h reminders so the organizer doesn't have to.
- Door mode: guest list on phone, mark arrivals (pre-ticketing utility that later becomes ticket scanning).

### 4. After the event: the proof and the loop
- **Report card email** (the keystone artifact): attendance signal, total reach, follower growth, "your audience is now 340 people," benchmark vs category average, one-tap "duplicate for next month."
- Post-event photo wall opens (Pankartat pattern generalized); attendee photos become next event's marketing.
- Feedback pulse to attendees ("how was it?") → private quality signal + public venue ratings eventually.

### 5. Growth (months): the ladder
- Verification ladder already exists (standard → established via the 2-events-in-90-days trigger → verified). Surface it everywhere; make "Verified organizer" a scene status symbol.
- Audience CRM: follower list, RSVP history, export. **Data portability as principle** — organizers stay because it works, not because they're trapped; this is also the anti-Eventbrite marketing line.
- Collab events (co-organizers, cross-promotion between organizers), venue-finder (browse venues with availability), volunteer recruitment from the /volunteer pool.
- When they're ready to charge: ticketing at 3–5% (vs Eventbrite's ~9% effective) with next-day payouts via Stripe Connect. By then AlbaGo has earned the transaction.

## What makes it indispensable (the lock-in stack, benevolent version)

1. **The follower graph** — leaves = abandoning their audience (the only real moat in creator platforms).
2. **The analytics history** — years of "what worked" data.
3. **The diaspora reach** — an Albanian promoter announcing a tour reaches Munich + London + NYC Albanians in one publish. Nobody else can sell this.
4. **The share-pack habit** — AlbaGo becomes their design department.
5. **The trust ladder** — verification status they earned.

## Concierge era (the honest part)

For the next two quarters, the "organizer platform" is partly a *service* wearing a product costume — and that's correct (Airbnb photographed apartments manually; DoorDash founders delivered food). Concretely:

- Personally onboard 20 named Tirana organizers: sit with them, create their first event together, WhatsApp support line.
- The team seeds venue pages (50 Tirana venues) so organizers pick from a real list instead of typing free-text.
- Weekly manual "editor's picks" — human curation substitutes for algorithms until data exists.
- Every manual act is research: what confuses them in the wizard is the UX backlog; what they ask for first is the feature roadmap; what they brag about is the marketing copy.

## Success metrics for this ecosystem

- **North star: weekly active organizers** (published or updated an event this week).
- Supply: events published/week per city; % recurring (habit signal).
- Love: organizer 90-day retention; % who publish a 2nd event within 30 days (the auto-promote trigger already measures this shape); NPS asked in the report-card email.
- Flywheel: average followers per organizer; % of event views arriving from follower notifications (the loop closing).
